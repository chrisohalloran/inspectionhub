# Professional source status and content boundary

Checked: 2026-07-15 (Australia/Brisbane)

## Current source status

- The current Queensland Building and Construction Commission Regulation 2018
  remains the governing licence-class source. Schedule 2, part 14 describes the
  completed residential building inspection scope by reference to AS 4349.1-2007.
  Source: <https://www.legislation.qld.gov.au/view/html/inforce/current/sl-2018-0138>
- AS 4349.1-2007 is therefore still the express Queensland licence-scope
  reference. The supplied licensed PDF was inspected for clause structure only.
  Standards Australia Store did not expose a directly indexable product-status
  page in this check, so the product must not claim that a Standards Australia
  catalogue status was independently confirmed.
- Standards Australia Store currently labels AS 4349.3-2010, _Inspection of
  buildings, Part 3: Timber pest inspections_, as `Pending Revision`.
  Source: <https://store.standards.org.au/explore-standards/construction?page=35>
- Recheck all three sources immediately before any real-customer activation.
  A replacement, amendment, licence-scope change or revised professional
  interpretation blocks publication of a new requirement-matrix version until
  reviewed by the appropriately licensed inspector.

## Product-content rule

The repository must not store or expose Standards clauses as product copy. It
may store:

- source identity, edition and clause references;
- independently worded workflow and report requirements;
- reviewer identity, review date, decision and version hash;
- evidence that an inspector approved the derived matrix and report content.

The source PDF remains outside the repository and is not a runtime dependency.
Any independently worded matrix is `draft_unverified` until the licensed
inspector records approval. Timber Pest content requires a separately competent
reviewer and never inherits Building approval.

## Professional boundary carried into the product

The Building inspection is a visual condition assessment at the inspection
time. The product must keep observations, inspector classification, limitations,
uncertainty and technical further-investigation recommendations distinct. It
must not turn the report into compliance certification, a warranty, a valuation,
rectification costing, legal advice, negotiation advice, settlement advice or a
buy/don't-buy signal.

Building and Timber Pest inspections may share evidence but require separate
taxonomies, conclusions, reviewer authority, immutable snapshots, approvals and
recipient report modules.
